§ I

What it does

Libretto, in two tongues

The full German libretto alongside an English translation, paginated for reading and aligned to the score so the text moves with the music.

Over 130 leitmotifs

Wagner's recurring musical figures — the Ring, the Sword, the Renunciation — surfaced in place as they sound, and browsable on their own as a thematic atlas.

Synced to the recording

Tap a line and the music meets you there. Tap a leitmotif and hear the moment it returns. Built on a measure-accurate index of the score.

Search & history

Find a phrase in either language and step through every occurrence. A history of where you've been, kept gently, in case you wish to return.

Synopses & scholarship

Plot summaries, character lists, and discussion of the music and its making — Wikipedia rendered in the app, no browser detour required.

An art gallery

A century of paintings, set designs, and engravings of the cycle — Rackham, Burne-Jones, Fantin-Latour and the rest — collected for unhurried viewing.

§ II

The recording

1958–65 Decca

Solti · Vienna Philharmonic

Launches with Sir Georg Solti's landmark studio cycle — the recording John Culshaw built across seven years at the Sofiensaal, and the one most listeners still measure the others against.

§ III

Apple Music required

The app does not ship with audio. It plays the cycle from your Apple Music subscription, which keeps the experience legal, lossless, and in your pocket. An active subscription is required.
